6. Data Sharing Within the Service

Teamglade is a collaboration platform where users work together in shared rooms. When you use our Service, certain information is shared with other users as follows:

Within Rooms: When you join or are invited to a room, your username and any files you upload become visible to all other members of that room. This is essential for collaboration purposes.

Topics and Messages: Any topics (messages) you create in a room, along with attached files, are visible to all room members. Messages are limited to 1000 characters.

File Uploads: Files you upload to a room are accessible to all members of that room. Files remain stored on our servers until you manually delete them.

Room Data: Topics, messages, and associated data remain in the room until the room is deleted by the room owner.

By participating in a room, you consent to sharing this information with other room members. Please do not share sensitive personal information in room messages or upload confidential files unless you trust all room members.

We will retain and use your Personal Data to the extent necessary to comply with our legal obligations (for example, if we are required to retain your data to comply with applicable laws), resolve disputes, and enforce our legal agreements and policies.

Specific retention periods for platform data:

User Account Data: Retained until you request account deletion or close your account.

Uploaded Files: Files are retained until you manually delete them from the room. When you delete a file, it is permanently removed from our servers.

Topics and Messages: Room messages, topics, and their associated data are retained until the room owner deletes the room. When a room is deleted, all associated topics and messages are permanently removed.

Backups: We do not maintain separate backup copies of deleted data. Once you delete files or a room owner deletes a room, the data is permanently removed and cannot be recovered.

We will also retain Usage Data for internal analysis purposes. Usage Data is generally retained for a shorter period, except when this data is used to strengthen the security or to improve the functionality of our Service, or we are legally obligated to retain this data for longer time periods.

The law's reach stretches well beyond California to require a person or company in the United States (and conceivable the world) that operates websites collecting personally identifiable information from California consumers to post a conspicuous privacy policy on its website stating exactly the information being collected and those individuals with whom it is being shared, and to comply with this policy.
